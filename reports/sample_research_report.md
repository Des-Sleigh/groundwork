# Research brief

**Question:** How are mid-market logistics firms using AI for demand forecasting, and what ROI evidence exists?

# AI Demand Forecasting in Mid-Market Logistics: A Synthesis Brief

## Executive Summary

Mid-market logistics firms are adopting AI demand forecasting through integrated, cloud-based platforms rather than standalone tools, and early evidence suggests they may reach ROI faster than large enterprises. Quantified performance gains are documented at the industry level, though firm-specific case studies remain sparse in the public record.

---

## 1. What Mid-Market Firms Are Adopting

### Technology Approach
The core AI toolkit in logistics demand forecasting centers on **machine learning and predictive analytics**, representing a deliberate departure from spreadsheet-driven planning and legacy Advanced Planning and Scheduling (APS) systems. These platforms compress decision cycles from weeks to minutes by integrating real-time data signals with probabilistic forecasting models.

Relevant vendor platforms operating at mid-market and enterprise tiers include **o9 Solutions, Kinaxis, Blue Yonder, Relex Solutions, Aera Technology, and E2open**, among others. Generative AI capabilities are also beginning to surface, either as add-ons to existing ERP systems or as privately hosted models.

### Integration Preference
The evidence suggests mid-market logistics firms favor **forecasting embedded within their operational platforms** over separate point solutions—a preference driven by simpler tech stacks and the practical difficulty of integrating standalone tools with existing workflows. This architectural choice is also a speed-to-value advantage (see Section 3).

## 3. Mid-Market vs. Enterprise: Barriers and Success Factors

This is arguably the most actionable dimension of the evidence base.

### Structural Advantages for Mid-Market Firms
Mid-market companies report reaching **AI ROI in 3–6 months**, compared to 12–24 months for large enterprises. Three factors drive this gap:
- **Simpler technology stacks** with fewer integration dependencies
- **Fewer decision-making layers**, enabling faster iteration
- **Ability to redesign workflows from scratch** rather than retrofit around legacy systems

Research indicates that the strongest predictor of reaching production deployment is not budget or model sophistication—it is **how few people can veto the build**.

### Shared Barriers Across Firm Sizes
- **Approval complexity:** Long approval chains are consistently associated with failed AI programs. MIT research found that 95% of corporate generative AI pilots produce no measurable P&L impact, with execution constraints—not appetite—as the binding factor.
- **Data quality:** Predictive analytics systems are only as good as the historical data feeding them. Incomplete or siloed operational data is a recurring implementation obstacle.
- **Build vs. buy risk:** Specialized vendor tools reach production deployment approximately 67% of the time; internal builds succeed roughly half as often—a meaningful difference for firms with limited AI engineering capacity.
