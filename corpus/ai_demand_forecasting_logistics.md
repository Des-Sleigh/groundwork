Mid-market logistics firms (roughly $50M–$500M revenue) increasingly apply
machine-learning demand forecasting to reduce safety stock and improve truckload
utilization. Common approaches include gradient-boosted trees and time-series
deep learning models that combine historical shipment volumes with external
signals such as weather, fuel prices, and seasonality.

Adoption typically starts with a narrow pilot on a single lane or product
category before expanding. Firms report that data quality work — cleaning
historical records and unifying systems — usually consumes more of the project
than modeling itself.

Forecast accuracy is most often measured with weighted MAPE against a held-out
period. Independent benchmarks are considered more reliable than vendor-reported
accuracy, which tends to be measured on favorable data.
